Kenyan Newspapers Review for August 27: GSU Officers Guarding Ruto's Karen Residence Hesitated to Ha

 Posted on May 29, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  725 words  |  Lourie Helzer

On Friday, August 27, the dailies reported extensively on the withdrawal of elite GSU officers from Deputy President William Ruto's residences.

The papers also reported a new radical plan had been hatched for the president to fire and hire the deputy president at his will.

1. Daily Nation

The publication reported there were tense moments after GSU officers manning Deputy President William Ruto's Karen residence hesitated to hand over to their AP counterparts.

The GSU elite guards were replaced with the administration police officers (Security of Government Buildings Unit (SGB), a move the Inspector General of Police Hillary Mutyambai termed normal.

When the AP officers arrived at the gate at about 3:22pm on Thursday, August 26, the GSU officers temporarily refused to let them in.

They were later allowed in and taken around the residence before they officially took over.

Ruto was at the time meeting a delegation from Nyeri led by Mathira MP Rigathi Gachagua, a close ally.

2. The Standard

Deputy President William Ruto's allies have protested the move by the government to withdraw the elite security at his residences.

The Tanga Tanga allied leaders termed the move unconstitutional.

Former National Assembly majority leader Aden Duale said the law that states the GSU commander must deploy officers to guard the president and his deputy must be followed.

"The security guarding the DP's residence in Nairobi and upcountry have been withdrawn! The political detractors of the DP must know there is law," Duale lamented.

Kimilili MP Didmus Barasa argued the move might scare future officeholders.

Elgeyo-Marakwet senator Kipchumba Murkomen questioned the decision and wondered why the president was after his deputy.

3. The Star

National Assembly Constitutional Implementation Oversight Committee chaired by Ndaragwa MP Jeremiah Kioni plans to introduce a new bill that, if passed in parliament, would see the president hire and fire his deputy at will.

This comes ata time when President Uhuru Kenyatta and his deputy, William Ruto, no longer see eye to eye with their relationship worsening daily.

The committee also proposed to expand the executive through the creation of a prime minister and two deputies positions.

"The rationale of the amendment is to ensure stability in government by ensuring the deputy president supports the agenda of the president," Kioni said.

The Uhuru allied lawmaker said the bill was not meant to target Ruto and could be voted for alongside 2022 polls.

4. People Daily

The Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) Secretariat has said it would team up with Attorney General Kihara Kariuki in appealing the Court of Appeal verdict that upheld the High Court's judgment declaring the bill unconstitutional, null and void.

According to Denis Waweru, who co-chairs the secretariat alongside Junet Mohamed, the judges in the High Court and their counterparts from the appellate court did not exhaustively deal with the respective cases brought before them.

"We believe that the two lower courts failed to deal with the delicate matters before them. We believe the Supreme Court has the capacity to address the issues," said Waweru.

Junet claimed the courts did not clarify the basic structure doctrine, which appeared to be the bone of contention during the appeal hearing in June.

He stated that the move to the apex court was motivated by the urge for further clarity on the matter.

"Kenyans deserve clarity on the Basic Structure Doctrine. We feel that as it is, the Judiciary has amended the Constitution of Kenya and written processes into it that Kenyans did not discuss or vote on,” stated Junet.

5. Taifa Leo

There was panic at Kalimamundu Primary School in Makueni after several students passed out due to hunger pants.

Kyudo education director Stephen Mulandi sought help from Kyuso High School so the pupils could have something to bite.

"I have been informed that several students fainted due to hunger occasioned by lack of food," Mulandi said in a letter addressed to Kyuso High School principal.

The education official attributed the lack of food to severe drought in Kitui county.
