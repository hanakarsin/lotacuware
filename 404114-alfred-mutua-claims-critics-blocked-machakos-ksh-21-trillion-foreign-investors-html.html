Governor Alfred Mutua has blamed his county political rivals and critics for failing to actualise his 2013 pledge to develop Machakos into a top world city.

The governor said his plan to build the city had gained momentum, but "anti-development" leaders went to court and blocked the multi-billion project.

Taking to Twitter on Thursday, February 25, Mutua said he had already invited investors who pledged KSh 2.1 trillion, but thanks to court cases, the project never materialised.

"In 2013, when I took office, I invited investors from around the world, and they pledged KSh 2.1 trillion for various investment projects, but unfortunately, anti-development leaders went to court and blocked the new city project," said Mutua.

Muthama and the Wiper party

In an interview with Citizen TV on September 27, 2020, the Maendeleo Chap Chap Chap leader claimed former Machakos Senator Johnstone Muthama, together with the Wiper Party, went to court to stop the city project.

“Johnstone Muthama, together with the Wiper party, went to court, and there was an injunction to stop the whole process until the case is heard and determined. The case took over 5 years before it was actually thrown out," said Mutua.

Despite the hurdles, the former government spokesman affirmed that the dream of putting up the city was still alive, with the first step being the construction of an airport.

The airport's runway, Mutua said, will also serve as the track for his Formula One circuit dream.

He, however, admitted that the remaining term would not be enough to complete the project hoping that the next governor who will come after him will carry on with the project.
