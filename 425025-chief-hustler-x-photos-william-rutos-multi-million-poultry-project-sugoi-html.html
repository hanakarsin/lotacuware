Chief Hustler: 5 Photos of William Ruto's Multi-Million Poultry Project in Sugoi

 Posted on May 5, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  461 words  |  Lourie Helzer
  • William Ruto often traces back his success from the days he sold chicken
  • It is little wonder that the chief hustler takes his chicken project seriously and it, in turn, brings him millions daily
  • A few years ago, during interviews with TV stations, Ruto and his wife Rachel gave Kenyans a glimpse of the multi-million business

A few days ago, Deputy President William Ruto made a damning revelation that he makes KSh 1.5 million daily in his chicken project and this left tongues wagging on social media.

But Ruto's poultry project in his rural Sugoi home is not your ordinary chicken business. A spot check by TUKO.co.ke showed the project is highly automated.

In a previous interview with Citizen TV, the second lady, Rachel Ruto, gave Kenyans a glimpse of the enterprise and many were wowed by the high mechanisation improvised in the project.

For instance, she explained the hundreds of thousands of the chicken are fed and watered using machines. The couple also has a way of collecting the chicken's droppings which they use as manure.

The Rutos poultry business also has an incubator that can hold 20,000 eggs, and a hatchery and they thus sell day-old chicks which Rachel said are sold on the exact day after the hatching process.

They also have an egg storage room that can be filled with a week's collection of eggs. She explained the project also helps her Joyful Women Organisation members who purchase the eggs and the birds.

Rachel disclosed the project was started with KSh 15 million.

"We also apply for tenders for supplying the day-old chicks. Like now the Uasin Gishu county government has advertised a tender," she said in the interview.

In another interview with K24, the deputy president also took Kenyans on a tour of his developed poultry house where he said the market was already beyond them.

At the time, he said his birds were around 5,000 but his recent revelation showed a fortyfold increase in the number of chickens.

"...the number of chickens I keep and the acreage of my farm; I have 200,000 chickens on my farm and I sell 150,000 eggs. From that I make KSh 1.5 million per day," he said on Thursday, September 2.

The DP said this in a rejoinder to Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i who had listed various properties linked to the deputy president.

He said Matiang'i mentioned the acreage of his Koitalel Poultry Farm but failed to disclose how much he makes.

In addition, the chicken seller politician added that much of his wealth comes from Safaricom dividends, where he holds 400,000 shares.
