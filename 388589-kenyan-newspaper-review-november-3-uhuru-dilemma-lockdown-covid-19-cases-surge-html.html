Kenyan newspaper review for November 3: Uhuru in dilemma on lockdown as COVID-19 cases surge

 Posted on May 15, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  766 words  |  Almeda Bohannan

The dailies on Tuesday, November 3, reported on the two-day Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) retreat that was held in Naivasha where some 300 MPs threw their weight behind the BBI.

The papers also widely reported on lawyer Paul Gicheru who surrendered himself to the International Court of Justice (ICC) five years after his arrest warrant was issued.

People Daily

Just a day to the much-waited meeting that President Uhuru Kenyatta is expected to hold with governors and health experts to review the COVID-19 situation in the country, one question lingers; to lock down the country or not?

The president is expected to make a tough balance on whether to close down the economy that had already started improving or leave Kenyans' lives in their own hands.

Speaking on Sunday, November 2, the head of state said he was pondering on the best measures to impose to curtail the growing number of infections and deaths.

Uhuru also blamed Kenyans for their lack of responsibility in observing the laid down COVID-19 guidelines.

"We are going through a difficult time where we are wondering what to do. Do we close up? We shall be coming to that soon," he told members of the All Saints Cathedral.

The Standard

More learners could be back to school as soon as next week after the Ministry of Education decided to reopen a few more classes, The Standard reports.

Education Cabinet Secretary George Magoha who hinted about the reopening said President Uhuru Kenyatta will chair a national consultative meeting which will kick off consensus building for reopening of more classes.

United Nations Children's Fund (Unicef)'s Maniza Zaman and Amref's Githinji Gitahi also supported the reopening of classes for the rest of the learners stating children are more at risk while out of school than in school.

This came three weeks after Grade Four, Class Eight and Form Four learners resumed learning seven months after schools closed in March 2020 when COVID-19 struck the country.

Daily Nation

According to Daily Nation, the surrender of Paul Gicheru at the International Court of Justice (ICC) could provide a leeway for the reopening of Deputy President William Ruto's case at the Hague.

The lawyer could strike a deal to testify against Ruto and Joseph Sang thereby providing fresh insights into the 2007/2008 election violence that left thousands dead and others displaced.

According to the representative of victims in the case Wilfred Nderitu, there is a possibility of the DP's case being reopened anew if any evidence proves he and Sang are guilty.

Gicheru is suspected of committing offences against the administration of justice by corruptly influencing witnesses who were set to provide evidence against the second in command.

Taifa Leo

According to Taifa Leo, President Uhuru Kenyatta and his handshake partner Raila Odinga have decided there will be no changes to the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI).

In a statement read by Siaya Senator James Orengo after a two-day BBI retreat in Naivasha, the lawmaker stated some 300 senators and MPs support the final report and there was no need to change it.

However, earlier, during the launching of the document, Uhuru and Raila had assured further changes will be made to the report to accommodate everyone's views.

Deputy President William Ruto has been saying some changes need to be adopted in the BBI so that it can speak for ordinary Kenyans.

The Star

According to the Star, the family of a man who was hurriedly buried after he was suspected of having COVID-19 has exhumed the body for a proper send-off.

The deceased, identified only as Clinton was buried on April 12 under strict COVID-19 rules on the suspicion had succumbed to the deadly virus.

His symptoms included a cough and he collapsed before he was rushed to the hospital and according to his family, the doctors tortured him by making him believe he had the virus.

Clinton will be buried in his ancestral home in Lugari, today, Tuesday, November 3.
