Sonkos Daughter Sandra Tells Gengetone Rapper Shalkido Not to Use Her Name for Clout

 Posted on March 21, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  392 words  |  Lourie Helzer
  • Mike Sonko's daughter Sandra has responded to Gengetone rapper Shalkido claiming they once dated
  • Shalkido had claimed he cut off his romance with Sandra after her father called him
  • However, Sandra denied ever meeting the rapper and warned him against using her name to chase clout

Sandra, the young daughter of former Nairobi Governor Mike Sonko, has told off Gengetone musician Shalkido who claimed they dated.

Speaking to a local publication, Shalkido, whose musical breakthrough was with the Sailors Gengetone group, claimed that Sandra’s influential dad had warned him against dating her.

Shalkido claimed that he received a phone call from the ousted Nairobi governor telling him to keep off his daughter.

“I used to like Sandra a lot but her father was not in for it. He called me one day and told me to stop it,” he claimed.

The rapper further alleged that he even met Sonko, and they settled the matter.

“I recently met him in Kitengela during a show and we cleared the air on that issue," he claimed.

Tracking phone

Speaking of his alleged relationship with Sandra, Shalkido said:

"We had started dating but it's like her dad used to track her phone. When he got wind of the affair I decided to move on."

Sandra responds

However, on Wednesday, September 8, Sandra laughed off Shalkido’s story and denied ever meeting the Wamlambez rapper.

"Wait, where is this coming from? Kenyans will kill me, of all people Shalkido,” she wrote.

The young lady further told him to stop using her name for publicity.

“This is someone I've never even met. Please don't use my name for clout. Find something to do,” Sandra stated.

Sandra tells off DJ

In August, TUKO.co.ke reported that Sandra told off a DJ who criticised her family’s Mua Hills mansion.

DJ 2One2 said that despite having money, the Sonko family mansion's colour and design was not pleasing to his eyes.

"Truth be told, you might have money but the colour and design is a no," wrote 2One2.

His sentiments caught the eye of Sandra, who responded, saying:

"Don’t you know it is located in Kamba land?"
