Well, in Ghana, the age limit to start university education is from 17 but Viemens has proven he has got the brain to start his tertiary education at the of age 12.

The 12-year-old has been offered a long-distance course in Public Administration.

He is the youngest among the over 2900 fresh students who were matriculated over the weekend by the university.

Being the second child in his family, Viemens was home-schooled by his father after completing his lower primary education in Akyem-Oda in the Eastern Region of the country.

He wrote and passed the West Africa Senior Secondary Certificate Examination (WASSCE) as a private candidate.

Asked how he managed to achieve this, his father, Robert Bamfo, said financial constraints made him choose this route and the fruits are better than he expected.

“What I teach first is dictionary skills so that the child is able to pronounce every word correctly," he said.“We do the phonetic skills and once that is done I teach Latin…for the sciences I do not give notes. What we do is textbook centered,” he added.

His elder brother, Edwin Bamfo, was also home schooled by his father after clearing his lower primary education.
