Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) secretary-general, Edwin Sifuna, has recalled the first time he travelled to Nairobi in search of a job.

The lawyer disclosed he carried his bed from the village to the city because he did not want to sleep on the floor, considering he had no money to purchase a new bed.

"My first journey to Nairobi can be described basically as a miracle. I came to the city with a bed because I wanted to avoid sleeping on the floor. I started life in South B on a staff quarters (SQ) house," Sifuna told Ghetto Radio.

The SG said after making several applications, he landed a job as a legal officer at Mini Bakeries Ltd, which is behind the production of Supa Loaf bread.

He thereafter proceeded to work at an advertising firm, but too much pressure at the workplace made him quit to start his own legal practice.

The lawyer said he joined ODM after he wrote a nice article about the Opposition outfit that caught the eyes of Kisumu governor Anyang' Nyong'o, who introduced him to the party's think thank.

Speaking to the same radio station, the senior ODM official revealed he met his wife in campus.

"I met my wife at the university even though she avoided me at the time. I didn't give up. We are blessed with a child aged four and half years.

Sifuna's ODM appointment

Sifuna was appointed ODM secretary-general in February 2018 by the party's national executive council under former prime minister Raila Odinga's leadership.

He took over from Agnes Zani, who served in an acting capacity after former Budalangi Member of Parliament Ababu Namwamba ditched the party, leaving a vacant position.

Since his appointment, the former Nairobi senatorial aspirant has been vocal in defending Raila and the Orange party.
