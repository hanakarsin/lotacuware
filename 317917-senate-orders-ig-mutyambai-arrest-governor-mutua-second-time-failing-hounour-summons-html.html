Senate orders IG Mutyambai to arrest Governor Mutua for second time after failing to hounour summons

 Posted on March 7, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  540 words  |  Hettie Henneman

- Governor Mutua has skipped the Senate summons thrice

- The Machakos leader is expected to respond to questions of financial impropriety in the 2017/2018 financial year as raised by the auditor general's report

- The governor has been questioning the independence of the Senate Committee on Public Accounts and Investment (CPAIC) claiming some members want to settle political scores

- Mutua recently accused Machakos Senator Boniface Kabaka of plotting to humiliate him for political reasons and demanded that he be excluded from the sitting

The Senate Committee on Public Accounts and Investment (CPAIC) has ordered Inspector General of Police Hillary Mutyambai to arrest Machakos Governor Alfred Mutua for evading to appear before it to respond to accountability questions.

This is the second time the senate is asking Mutyambai to arrest the Machokos leader who has snubbed the summons three times.

In a statement issued by PAIC chair Moses Kanjwang on Tuesday, October 8, senators asked the IG to arrest Mutua and present him before the committee to answer to questions of financial impropriety in the 2017/2018 financial year.

Last week, the governor failed to show up at a senate sitting in Kitui where he was scheduled to appear and respond to the questions as raised in the auditor's report.

In his defence, Mutua claimed he feared to attend the sitting because Machakos Senator Boniface Kabaka had organised a group of youth to heckle and humiliate him at the venue.

He requested the committee to reschedule the meeting and consider excluding Kabaka from the sitting claiming the senator was biased and would engage in political sideshows rather than objectively evaluating the financial records.

“Senator Kabaka should not be allowed to use an innocent committee sitting to advance personal interests as this obviously amounts to blackmail, arm-twisting and abuse of office,” he stated in a letter to the committee

The governor was then ordered to appear before the committee on October 8 but failed to show up for the third time.

Mutua is out of the country for officials duty and had requested the watchdog committee to allow his deputy, Francis Maliti, who is also the finance executive, to appear on his behalf.

He accused the committee of undermining his leadership and attempting to portray him as a corrupt leader

"I am currently out of the country and surprised that even after informing the Senate PAIC committee of the same a while back, they have again secretly met without notice to me or my government and continued to perpetuate an agenda to show me in a bad light even though they know I am away," Mutua said.

The Machakos leader has become the second governor to face the committee's warrant of arrest after Kakamega's Wycliffe Oparanya who equally failed to honour summons.
