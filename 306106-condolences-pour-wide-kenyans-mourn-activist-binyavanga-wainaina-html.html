Binyavanga Wainaina: Celebrities pay tribute to fallen Kenyan LGBT activist, veteran author

 Posted on June 21, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  393 words  |  Hettie Henneman

- Wainaina died at the Aga Khan Hospital in Nairobi on the night of Tuesday, May 21

- The blogger was a renowned and award winning African author who was celebrated by many

- Binya as he was popularly known bagged the 2002 Caine Prize winner for African Writing

- His death came a few days after he announced plans to wed his long time Nigerian lover

The death of Kenyan gay rights activist Binyavanga Wainaina has attracted overwhelming reactions from both the local and the international community.

Among those who paid tribute to the fallen award winning African author who died on the night of Tuesday, May 21, at Aga Khan Hospital, Nairobi, were celebrities, activists and authors.

Some of the prominent personalities who sent their condolences were Kenyan-born BBC reporter Larry Madowo who described the deceased as a public intellectual, and Kenyan author and journalist Oyunga Pala who remembered Wainaina for his audacity and contributions.

"Binyavanga Wainaina was the public intellectual we needed but didn't deserve. The world is worse off today without him to challenge our prejudices and defend the humanity of everyone. And now his watch is ended," Madowo tweeted on Wednesday morning."I remember the early days, from SA to Kwani? open mic at Yaya. Audacity of possibilities. You started something special. You made important contributions to our writing space. You lived your truth. Now go in peace bro," Oyunga said.

Wainaina, the founder of literary magazine Kwani?, reportedly died after a short illness.

His family indicated the 48-year-old activist died of stroke.

According to one of his friends, Michael Olweny, the activist first suffered a stroke back in 2016.

Olweny said Wainaina was set to do a documentary on homosexuality and always asked Kenyans to speak openly on the thorny issue.
