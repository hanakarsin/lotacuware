Amanda Gorman: Young poet gets over 1M followers after performing at Biden's inauguration

 Posted on June 10, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  591 words  |  Enda Jantzen

- Amanda Gorman stole the show at President Joe Biden's inauguration ceremony with her powerful performance

- The 22-year old poet who recited The Hill we Climb became the youngest inaugural poet in the US history

- Amanda who dazzled fans with her mastery of words got more than one million new followers in less than 24 hours on her Instagram account

A 22-year-old poet Amanda Gorman had a special role during the US 46th President's swearing ceremony reciting The Hill We Climb poem.

Her graceful and eloquent performance earned her a place in the heart of many people globally, who followed her across her social media platforms.

The 22-year-old was already a household name in the creatives industry, but the majority will remember her as the brave, confident young poet who called for unity during the swearing-in of the 46th president of the United States.

Her performance brought a light moment during the inauguration event, leaving most people tweeting her lines and following her on social media.

Before she took the podium, she had at least 886K followers, however, after her performance, everyone wanted to know who this poet who stole the show was, and by this morning, she had 2.1M followers on Instagram.
