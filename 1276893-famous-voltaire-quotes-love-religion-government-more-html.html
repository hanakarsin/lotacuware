Famous Voltaire quotes on love, religion, government and more

 Posted on August 5, 2024  |  9 minutes  |  1760 words  |  Hettie Henneman

As one of the greatest French Enlightenment writers, François-Marie d'Arouet (1694–1778), who was better known by his pen name “Voltaire,” was a prolific writer and witty philosopher. He dedicated his life to advocating for social concerns on critical issues in the society, such as religious dogma, French government establishments of the time, and the intolerance in the community. Many years after his death, Voltaire quotes seem to reflect on what is happening in society.

Just like most Enlightenment philosophers before him, Voltaire was an outspoken advocate for civil liberties. He addressed issues concerning freedom of expression, freedom of religion, criticism of Christianity, to mention a few. Most of the best Voltaire quotes are available through his literary works and platforms that range from poems, essays, novels, historical documentation, scientific research, and many more.

Below is a compilation of the most famous Voltaire quotes that are sure to captivate your critical thinking abilities while offering you a variety of creative and witty quotes to apply in your day to day conversations.
