Garissa Town MP Aden Duale hints at retirement from politics if Ruto becomes president

 Posted on July 26, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  405 words  |  Hettie Henneman

- MP Aden Duale said he would be available to provide counsel to William Ruto if he becomes head of state

- The Leader of Majority in the National Assembly claimed he had left a legacy in his constituency

- The country's second in command has declared his ambitions to succeed his boss Uhuru when he completes his second and final term in 2022

Garissa Town Member of Parliament Aden Duale has hinted at retiring from politics if Deputy President William Ruto assumes power in the 2022 General Election.

The Leader of Majority in the National Assembly who is a die-hard Ruto supporter revealed he will be ready to hang his political boots in the event his close friend becomes president.

In an interview with Citizen TV on Monday, December 30, the vocal lawmaker said he would be available to provide counsel to Ruto if need be.

“Huyo hustler akiingia mimi ata nikiwa mtu wa kunywa chai hapo ikulu kama mzee wa mtaa ni sawa ( If the hustler (Ruto) becomes president, I will not mind drinking evening tea with him as an advisor),” he said. "I do not want to die a politician, it is the worst death. In politics it is good to leave when people still love you. I have left a legacy in my constituency," he added.

Duale said Jubilee was the party to watch for the next 50 years and might break KANU's 40-year rule record.

"Jubilee is a party to watch in the next 50 years, the people you see now most of them are first timers; they are toddlers, they are still wearing political napkins," he said.

The country's second in command has declared his intentions to succeed his boss Uhuru Kenyatta when he retires after his second and final term in 2022.

The former higher education minister has been criss-crossing the country in search for votes in his first attempt to occupy State House.
