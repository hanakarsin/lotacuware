Big win for Uhuru, Raila as over 24 county assemblies pass BBI bill

- A total of 28 county assemblies had debated and endorsed the document as on Tuesday, February 23

- Makueni senator Mutula Kilonzo said Kenyans should expect a robust debate in both the Senate and the National Assembly

The fate of the Building Bridges Initiative bill now lies squarely in the hands of the members of the National Assembly and the Senate after over 24 county assemblies passed it.

As on Tuesday, February 23, a total of 28 county assemblies had debated and endorsed the document which paved way for the next stage of the constitutional amendment.

This means that the BBI endorsement passed the 24 counties mark, a constitutional threshold required for the document to formally be introduced in the Senate and the National Assembly.

The proposed amendment will be forwarded to the president for assent if a majority of the members of each House approve it.

But the same will be submitted to the people in a referendum if either House of Parliament fails to pass the bill, or if the bill relates to a matter specified in Article 255 (1) of the Constitution.

Under Article 255(1), some areas of the Constitution must be subjected to a referendum if they are to be changed.

The bill may be introduced in either House and a second reading of the proposed amendments done after 90 days following the first reading in that House.

It will be considered to have passed when each House pass it in both its second and third readings by not less than two-thirds of all the members of that House.

According to Senator Mutula Kilonzo, both houses will have to set up a joint committee to avoid waste of time.

"Kenyans should expect a robust debate in Parliament. Leaders of both houses should set up a joint committee to help in a joint public participation," he told TUKO.co.ke.

Murang'a, Nyandarua, Nyeri, Garissa, Nyamira, Machakos, Nakuru, Meru, Makueni, Narok, Tharaka Nithi and Kakamega became the latest to join the growing list of BBI yes-counties.
