Laban Mosi, a survivor of the 2013 Westgate attack, has revealed he has avoided church and crowded places for eight years due to paranoia.

The civil and structural engineer said he only attended church during the funeral services of his father and uncle, who lost their lives in two weeks.

The services were held at Gendia Seventh Day Adventist church in Homa Bay, far away from Nairobi, a crowded city that made him nervous.

Mosi said his fear for crowded places started after the Westgate attack, which he managed to escape narrowly.

He recalled arriving at the mall on Saturday, September 21, for a scheduled meeting with a client, which was never to be.

Immediately he arrived at the parking lot; his wife called him asking about his whereabouts.

"I told her I was at Westgate, as I was just about to park my car. She immediately laughed and asked me cheekily to just confirm where I truly was,” Daily Nation quoted Mosi.

She informed him about the attack broadcasted on local media, and he soon heard gunshots rending the air.

"I'm still struggling to piece together how I escaped from the parking bay. There was heavy and deafening gunshots, but somehow I escaped, and that's where my problems with paranoia started," he recalls.

He said he was lucky to refuse his wife's company, who was six months pregnant at the time.

"I was also grateful that I disapproved of my wife's request to accompany me that morning."

Mosi managed to get home and followed the terror attack on TV, but his client was stuck at the mall but was safe.

The 35-year-old man said his fear for public places increased following the Garissa University terror attack that led to the deaths of 148 people majority of whom were students.

He said he was traumatised after people were shot dead in the venue he was to hold a meeting.

"For a while I kept hearing the gunshots in my sleep but they stopped," he said.

Westgate's reopening

The Westgate mall in Nairobi re-opened its doors officially on Saturday, July 18, for shoppers, 22 months later since the terror attack that left 67 Kenyans and foreigners dead.
