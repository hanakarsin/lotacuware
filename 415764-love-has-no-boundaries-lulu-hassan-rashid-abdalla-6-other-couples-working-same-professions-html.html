In the Kenyan Showbiz industry, we have several couples working in the same professions, from TV to music to the comedy industry.

Here is a list of seven couples thriving in the same profession.

1. Jemutai and Hamo

They are both in the laugh industry and have featured on Churchill Show several times. Comedienne Jemutai is Hamo's second wife, and they have two children.

2. Lulu Hassan and Rashid Abdalla

The media power couple is the most loved pair in the country. Lulu and her hubby are employed at Royal Media Services owned Citizen TV. They are co-hosts and present news every weekend.

3. Wahu Kagwi and Nameless

Their love brewed 23 years ago when they were still studying at the University of Nairobi. They are both musicians, and their journey hasn't been a smooth one.

Nameless and Wahu have been married for 15 years and are among the top musicians in the country.

4. Eddie Butita and Eunice Mammito

The lovebirds are some of the creative minds in the laugh industry. They are known for their funny jokes and were nurtured by the Godfather of Kenyan comedy Daniel "Churchill" Ndambuki.

They featured on Churchill Show for quite a while.

5. Njugush and Celestine Ndinda aka Wakavinye

They are content creators and are good at what they do. The couple has a huge fan base on social media, where they share their content and get thousands of views. Their four-year-old son also owns a YouTube channel, where they share his videos.

6. Kate Actress and Phil Karanja

They are both actors. They met when Karanja was acting in Tahidi High school drama and Kate on Mother-in-law show as the lead character.

Media power couples

The media industry is fast-paced, especially in the modern world, where news breaks and is spread on social media via a click of a button.

More than ever, this has put journalists on their toes as they try to keep up with the minute-by-minute events and occurrences across the globe.

Therefore, it follows that when looking for a partner to start a family with, they understand the crazy schedules, calls at the wee hours of the night, and impromptu assignments.
