Her illustrious career

It requires passion, courage, and a certain degree of intelligence for one to succeed in a career they did not pursue in college. Kamene is one example of such people. Although she holds a degree in Law, she has managed to conquer the airwaves in an exemplary fashion. But how did this come to be?

Television career

While in her third year at the University of Nairobi, she got a chance to try out for a screen test on TV. This encounter would mark the genesis of her illustrious media career. At age 21, Kamene Goro was a Prime Time news anchor and reporter at the fast-growing media station - Ebru TV.

She left the TV station in September 2015 but later rejoined the company in 2017 and assumed the role of head of news. However, she left the station in late 2017. Some of the exciting television shows that she hosted while at Ebru TV include Just the Facts, The Weekly Report, and Africa in Brief.

Radio career

Kamene then joined NRG Radio breakfast show, where she co-hosted with the controversial Andrew Kibe. In June 14th, 2019, the pair moved to Kiss 100 before Kibe resigned and was replaced by Jalang'o. Currently, the media personality co-hosts the Morning Kiss Show alongside Phelix Odiwour alias Jalang'o.

Before joining the media industry, Kamene worked as a paralegal at Simani & Co Advocate from February 2013 to July 2013. She then developed an interest in media and never looked back. However, she remains open and lives by God's guidance. She still loves the legal world, and if fate takes her back there, then she will gladly accept.

However, she admits that she loves where she is in life at the moment. Besides being a radio presenter, she is also an emcee, product promoter, and event host.

Like most media personalities, Kamene's career has not been a comfortable ride. Numerous controversies have marred it. However, the fact that she manages to shake most of them off and move on with her career is something that most people love her for.

Her unceremonial departure from NRG

In July 2019, Miss Goro and her co-host Andrew Kibe made headlines when they unceremoniously left NRG (Energy) Radio to join Kiss 100. As a result, both celebrities were sued by their former employer over a non-compete clause in their contracts.

The two resigned from NRG Radio and paid their ex-employer three months salary in place of issuing a notice. However, according to the NRG application statement to the court, the period of three months was reasonable to restrain their former employees considering the substantial investment that had been made on them.
