Kilifi woman gifted new house by Governor Amason Kingi after she delivered quadruplets

 Posted on August 13, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  559 words  |  Hettie Henneman

- Kilifi Governor Amason Kingi delivered on a promise he made to a couple who were blessed with quadruplets

- Kingi had promised to build a new house for the couple and to fulfil his promise, he handed over keys to a new house to the couple

- The two had been living in deplorable conditions after their house was destroyed by heavy rains in December 2019

- The parents, of now 7 children, were blessed with quadruplets in March 2020

- The baby girls were born weighing 1.29kg, 1.01kg, 0.93kg and 0.97kg, after which they were admitted to the HDU

Kilifi Governor Amason Kingi on Friday, May 8, 2020, delivered on a promise he made to the family of Kombe Charo Kazungu and his wife Mishi Kitsao after they were blessed with quadruplets.

The couple had been living in deplorable conditions after their house was destroyed by heavy rains in December 2019.

Kingi on April 19, 2020, promised to build a new house for the couple and to fulfil his promise handed over keys to a new house to the couple on May 8, 2020.

"I have officially handed Kombe Charo Kazungu and his wife Mishi Kitsao the keys to their new house as I had promised," Governor Kingi wrote on his Facebook page"The lovely parents, of now 7 children, were blessed with quadruplets about a month ago and I committed to building a new, better house for them," he added.

This was in order to ensure the family had a better roof to live in with regards to the current weather condition and the COVID-19 pandemic.

The baby girls were born weighing 1.29kg, 1.01kg, 0.93kg and 0.97kg, after which they were admitted to the High Dependency Unit (HDU) ward at the Kilifi Sub-county Hospital.

Nurses at the facility had also raised concerns that the lives of the quadruplets would be at risk if they had gone back to a ruined house.

"We have also provided the family with essential products and we will continue to monitor the quadruplets as we engage the parents in a sustainable income-generating activity," Kingi said."We wish the family, especially the newborn babies, good health and that the family resumes normalcy in the shortest time possible," Kingi added.

The governor expressed gratitude to well-wishers who supported the family by making contributions despite the difficult times they were going through.

Kitsao's plight was highlighted by NTV prompting the governor to ensure the mother and the babies had a roof over their heads when they are discharged.

He also promised to provide food to the family for three months. The house will have a toilet and water harvesting facilities.

Kitsao's husband does menial jobs in Mombasa. Many places where he used to get jobs have been closed due to the coronavirus.
