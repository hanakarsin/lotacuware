Queen Elizabeth II receives Manoah Esipisu as Kenya's High Commissioner to UK

 Posted on March 13, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  354 words  |  Hettie Henneman

Kenya's High Commissioner to the UK Manoah Esipisu has met Queen Elizabeth and presented his credentials following his July 2018 appointment by President Uhuru Kenyatta.

The former State House spokesperson met with the queen at Buckingham Palace on Tuesday, March 26.

In his tweet seen by TUKO.co.ke, on Tuesday, March 26, Esipisu confirmed presenting the letter of credence to Her Majesty Elizabeth II where he thanked her for playing a key role in sustaining and enhancing cordial working relationship between UK and Kenya.

"Presented my letters of credence, by which President Uhuru Kenyatta appointed me High Commissioner to the UK, to Her Majesty Queen Elizabeth II at Buckingham Palace. Thanked the Queen for her enduring friendship with Kenya," he said.

In an affirming tweet, the royal family confirmed receiving his credence letter.

"Today The Queen received His Excellency Manoah Esipisu, High Commissioner for the Republic of Kenya in London," read a tweet by the royal family.

Esipisu was appointed alongside former Salaries and Remuneration Commission (SRC) chairperson Sarah Serem who was assigned to represent Kenya in China.

In the appointments, former director of National Intelligence Service (NIS) Chris Karumba Mburu was nominated to be Kenya’s ambassador to South Sudan while former Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) boss Ndegwa Muhoro was sent to Malasyia in the same capacity.

Samuel Nganga Thuita was placed in Israel while former Ainamoi MP Benjamin Langat was appointed to represent the country in Namibia.

Former Kasipul Kabondo MP Paddy Ahenda was sent to Qatar while Johnson Kimani Ondieki was appointed Kenya's ambassador to Turkey.
