What is the cut off mark for Nasarawa State University?

Like any higher learning institution, NSUK can only admit eligible students. Thus, you must ensure you fulfil the minimum requirements before applying for your preferred course.

JAMB usually releases a cut-off mark every year. The cut-off mark for the 2020/2021 academic year was 160. It is essential to note that the institution is corruption-free. Therefore, any student who does not attain this requirement will not be considered during the screening exercise. Also, the mark can vary depending on the course one is seeking to pursue.

The institution of higher learning started its 2021/2022 Post-UTME screening on December 1, 2021, and will end on December 32, 2021. All interested candidates are supposed to register through the university’s registration portal.

Is Nasarawa University form out?

It seems like the entry forms for the 2021/2022 academic year are not yet out as the online portal is yet to allow processing for the period. Usually, the UTME form is out early to enable screening, which takes place between August and October. For instance, in 2019, the exercise began on August 13 and ended on October 25.

The university extended the deadline for the 2020/2021 applications/screening exercise to January 31, 2021. The process started on November 23, 2021

Registered students can log on to the NSUK portal for further announcements. They might have been delayed due to the rising cases of COVID-19, which saw learning suspended for some time.

How much is NSUK admission form? The Nasarawa State University Post UTME form costs N2,000 (Two thousand naira only).
